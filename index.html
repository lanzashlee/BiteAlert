Animal Bite Symptoms

Recognizing the signs of an animal bite is crucial for timely treatment. Common symptoms include pain, swelling, redness, and bleeding at the bite site. More serious signs may include fever, headache, muscle weakness, and difficulty swallowing.

First Aid for Animal Bites

If bitten, immediately wash the wound with soap and water for at least 15 minutes. Apply pressure to stop bleeding and cover with a clean bandage. Seek medical attention immediately for proper wound care and vaccination. Remember, rabies is preventable if treated promptly.

Prevention & Safety

Stay safe by avoiding contact with stray animals, keeping pets vaccinated, and teaching children proper animal interaction. Report stray animals to local authorities and maintain a safe distance from unfamiliar animals. Prevention is the best protection against animal bites.
